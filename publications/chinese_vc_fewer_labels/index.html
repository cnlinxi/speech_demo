Chinese Audio samples of "Voice Conversion with Fewer Labels by Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks"

Abstract: Recent studies have shown remarkable success in voice conversion (VC) based on generative adversarial networks (GANs) without parallel data. This paper proposes a semi-supervised method based on conditional generative adversarial network (CGAN) which allows non-parallel many-to-many voice conversion with fewer labeled data. In the proposed method, the discriminator of GAN not only learns to distinguish real/fake samples, but also learns to classify samples. The discriminator is used to predict labels for the unlabeled real samples which reduces the reliance on labeled data. By following the approach, we only need to provide speech with a small part of labeled data, which enable a single generation network to implement many-to-many mapping between different voice domains and generate realistic audio as quickly as real time. In particular, the proposed approach is able to achieve comparable voice quality and speaker similarity with other methods by using only 10% of the labeled data.
